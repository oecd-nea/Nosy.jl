Examples

Nosy is unit-agnostic: the only requirement is self-consistency. The examples below assume MW for power and capacity, MWh for energy, EUR/MW for fixed costs, EUR/MWh for variable costs, and tons for masses and CO2.

The numerical assumptions in these examples are illustrative only. They are chosen to keep the examples compact and easy to interpret; they are not NEA reference values and should not be read as technology-cost assumptions. For actual technology cost data, see the NEA/IEA Projected Costs of Generating Electricity reports and data resources.
